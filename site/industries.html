Industries We Serve

Getiva Solutions serves clients across key sectors of the US economy. Our recruiters and delivery teams understand the regulatory, technical, and operational context of each industry.

Financial Services

Banks, asset managers, insurers, and fintechs rely on IT for security, compliance, and customer experience. We place talent in areas such as core banking systems, risk and compliance technology, cybersecurity, data engineering, and digital channels. We are familiar with the need for background checks, confidentiality, and fast time-to-fill in a regulated environment.

Typical roles: Application developers, security engineers, data analysts, project managers, infrastructure and cloud engineers.

Healthcare

Healthcare organizations need technology that supports patient care, privacy, and interoperability. We staff roles in EHR and clinical systems, health analytics, interoperability and integration, and IT security—with an understanding of HIPAA and healthcare IT norms. We help you build and run teams that can deliver in a high-stakes, regulated setting.

Typical roles: Clinical applications support, health IT analysts, integration developers, cybersecurity, project and program managers.

Technology

Software companies, SaaS providers, and technology-driven businesses need strong engineering and product talent. We support product development, platform engineering, DevOps, cloud, and technical leadership. We speak the language of agile, product roadmaps, and modern tech stacks—and we know how to assess and place technical talent at pace.

Typical roles: Software engineers, DevOps/SRE, product managers, technical leads, QA and automation engineers.

Retail & eCommerce

Retail and eCommerce depend on e-commerce platforms, supply chain systems, and customer-facing digital experiences. We help you staff for peak seasons, new market launches, and ongoing platform and analytics work. Our placements support both legacy and modern commerce technology.

Typical roles: E-commerce developers, supply chain and logistics systems analysts, data and analytics, UX and digital experience, project managers.

Manufacturing

Manufacturing is increasingly driven by IT—from shop-floor systems and IoT to ERP, supply chain, and quality systems. We place talent who understand operational technology, integration, and the need for reliability and uptime. We support digital transformation and continuous improvement initiatives.

Typical roles: ERP and MES specialists, integration and automation engineers, data and analytics, IT project managers, infrastructure and security.

We serve clients across the United States in these and adjacent sectors.
